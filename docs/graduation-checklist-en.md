## 🗺️ The whole process

```
① Check eligibility ─▶ ② Apply for degree exam ─▶ ③ Oral defense ─▶ ④ Revise thesis
                          (Nov 30 / Apr 30)                            │
                                                                       ▼
⑧ Collect diploma ◀─ ⑦ Apply for clearance ◀─ ⑥ Submit print + ◀─ ⑤ Upload e-thesis
                       (clearance system)       authorization         (→ Library review)
```

## ① Check your eligibility

| Status | Minimum semesters enrolled | Other requirements |
|---|---|---|
| Master's student | at least the **2nd semester** | Completed (or completing this semester) all required courses and credits |
| Doctoral student | at least the **4th semester** | Same, **plus a passed qualifying examination** |
| Direct-track PhD | at least the **4th semester** in the doctoral program | Same |

> If your master's program has its own qualifying examination, you must pass that too.

## ② Apply for the degree examination (學位考試)

**Deadlines (fixed every year)**

| Semester | Application period |
|---|---|
| 1st semester | From the first day of classes to **November 30** |
| 2nd semester | From the first day of classes to **April 30** |

> Exceptions require approval from your advisor and your department chair / institute
> director / degree program director.

**Documents to submit to your department**
- Academic transcript (歷年成績表)
- Thesis draft and abstract (論文初稿及提要)
- **Degree Examination Application Form** (學位考試申請書), signed by your advisor

**Apply at:** https://gra103.aca.ntu.edu.tw/degree/index_main.asp

**Withdrawing:** submit a **Withdrawal Application** (撤銷申請書) before the last day of the
semester per the academic calendar, signed by your advisor and department head.
⚠️ **If you neither withdraw nor sit the examination in time, it counts as one failure.**

## ③ Oral defense ④ Revise your thesis

Revise per your committee's comments. Only the finalized version enters the submission process.

## ⑤ Upload your electronic thesis

1. Read the **NTU Electronic Thesis Upload Manual** (臺大電子學位論文上傳手冊) first
2. Log in to the **NTU Thesis Submission System** (臺灣大學博碩士論文提交系統) and enter your thesis metadata
3. Upload the full-text PDF **with watermark, DOI, and security settings applied**
4. Choose who your full text is licensed to (the university, the National Central Library, database vendors)

**Library review time**
- Each review is completed within **2 working days** (counted from the day after submission, excluding weekends and holidays)
- ⚠️ **In the 2 weeks before the deadline (peak season) this can stretch to 4–5 working days** — build in buffer

### Delayed public release (embargo)
If you need an embargo for patents or confidentiality, file the
**Delayed Release Application and Review Form** (延後公開申請書及審核表).

> Under Article 9 of the Regulations for Master's and Doctoral Degree Examinations,
> **not filing the form means immediate public release** — your thesis will be openly
> available at the NTU Library and the National Central Library.

## ⑥ Submit printed copies and authorization forms

### Number of copies and where to submit

| Department | Copies | Submit to |
|---|---|---|
| Most departments | **2** | Main Library |
| Mathematics, Physics, **Chemistry**, Oceanography, College of Social Sciences, College of Law | **3** | Main Library |
| College of Medicine, College of Public Health | **2** | Medical Library |

### Required components of the printed thesis
- Cover (**including the spine information**)
- Title page (same content as the cover)
- Oral examination committee certification (**signed**)
- Body text (**with watermark and DOI**)

> 💡 This template generates the cover, title page, and spine for you — see the
> [README](../README.md); the spine compiles separately from `bookspine.tex`.
> The watermark and DOI are added by the **submission system**, not by you in LaTeX.

### Authorization forms
- **3 copies** total (NTU, database vendors, National Central Library)
- After your review passes, the forms arrive by email — **print all 3 and sign them by hand**
- Submit the **originals**, not photocopies

## ⑦ Apply for graduation clearance

1. Log in to the [Graduation Clearance System](https://my.ntu.edu.tw/studleave/) and click **"申請清查"** (Apply for clearance)
2. **Return all library materials** borrowed from any NTU library branch, cancel all holds, and settle any outstanding fees
3. Submit the **Declaration of Academic Ethics and Originality** (學位論文學術倫理暨原創性聲明書) to your department for record

> There is **no fixed deadline** for completing clearance — you can pace it yourself.
> But you cannot collect your diploma until it is done.

## ⑧ Collect your diploma

**Three requirements before a diploma is issued**
1. Passing grades in required courses and the oral examination, and meeting your department's graduation requirements
2. Thesis registered in the submission system with the electronic full text uploaded
3. Printed copies submitted to the Library

Only when **every** item on the clearance checklist is complete may you collect your diploma
from the academic affairs office.

> 📌 Graduating students who need Chinese or English transcripts must apply
> **the day after** collecting the diploma.

## ✅ Self-check list

```
□ Eligibility met (semesters / credits / PhD qualifying exam)
□ Degree examination application submitted (before Nov 30 or Apr 30)
□ Oral defense passed; thesis revised per committee comments
□ E-thesis uploaded to the submission system (watermark / DOI / security)
□ Library review passed (allow 4–5 working days in peak season)
□ Embargo application filed (if needed — no form means immediate release)
□ 3 authorization forms printed and hand-signed
□ Printed copies ready (right number? signed certification page? spine?)
□ Printed copies + original authorization forms submitted (before Aug 25)
□ All library materials returned, no outstanding fees
□ Academic ethics and originality declaration submitted to department
□ Clearance application submitted and all items cleared
□ Diploma collected
```
